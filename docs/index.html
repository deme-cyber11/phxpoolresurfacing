Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about pool resurfacing in Phoenix

How much does pool resurfacing cost in Phoenix? +
Pool resurfacing typically costs $4,000-$15,000 depending on pool size and finish type. Basic plaster runs $4-6 per square foot, while premium Pebble Tec costs $8-12 per square foot. We provide free estimates with exact pricing for your pool.
How long does pool resurfacing take? +
Most pool resurfacing projects take 5-7 days from start to finish. This includes draining, surface preparation, applying the new finish, filling, and initial chemical balancing. Weather can occasionally affect timing.
When is the best time to resurface a pool in Arizona? +
October through March is ideal. Cooler temperatures allow for better curing of the new surface, and you won't miss prime swimming season. However, we resurface pools year-round with proper precautions.
How long does pool plaster last? +
Standard pool plaster lasts 7-12 years in Arizona's harsh climate. Pebble Tec and quartz finishes last 15-20+ years due to their superior durability against sun exposure and hard water.
Pebble Tec vs plaster - which is better? +
Pebble Tec costs more upfront ($8-12/sq ft vs $4-6/sq ft for plaster) but lasts twice as long and requires less maintenance. For Arizona pools exposed to intense sun and hard water, Pebble Tec often provides better long-term value.
Can you resurface a pool with water in it? +
No, the pool must be completely drained for resurfacing. We handle the draining, and proper drainage is important to avoid damaging the pool structure from hydrostatic pressure.

Plaster Pool Resurfacing

Classic white plaster finish - affordable, timeless, and proven

About Plaster Resurfacing

Pool plaster has been the industry standard for decades. This cement-based finish provides a smooth, classic look that complements any backyard. White plaster reflects sunlight, keeping your pool water sparkling and inviting.

In Arizona's climate, standard plaster typically lasts 7-12 years with proper maintenance. It's the most affordable resurfacing option and works well for pool owners who want a clean, traditional appearance.

Benefits of Plaster

  • Affordable: Lowest cost per square foot of any pool finish
  • Classic look: Timeless white appearance that never goes out of style
  • Smooth texture: Comfortable on bare feet
  • Easy to repair: Patches blend well if needed
  • Proven durability: Decades of proven performance

Plaster Resurfacing Pricing

Large pool (600 sq ft): $2,400 - $3,600

When to Choose Plaster

Plaster is ideal if you're on a budget, prefer the classic white pool look, or plan to sell your home in the next few years. It's also a good choice for rental properties where maximum ROI matters.

For pools that see heavy use or if you want a longer-lasting finish, consider upgrading to Pebble Tec or quartz.

Pebble Tec & Aggregate Finishes

Premium durability and natural beauty for Arizona pools

Why Pebble Tec?

Pebble Tec is the gold standard for pool finishes in Arizona. This aggregate finish combines small pebbles with cement to create an incredibly durable surface that stands up to our harsh sun, hard water, and extreme temperatures.

Unlike standard plaster that may need replacing in 7-12 years, Pebble Tec routinely lasts 15-20+ years. The natural pebble texture also hides minor imperfections and provides excellent slip resistance.

Benefits of Pebble Tec

  • Superior durability: Lasts 15-20+ years in Arizona conditions
  • Stain resistant: Better resistance to mineral staining and algae
  • Natural beauty: Unique pebble texture with depth and character
  • Color options: Wide range of pebble blends to match your style
  • Lower maintenance: Requires less chemical balancing than plaster
  • Better value: Higher upfront cost but lower lifetime cost

Quartz Pool Finishes

Sparkling beauty with excellent durability

About Quartz Finishes

Quartz pool finishes combine crusite quartz aggregate with white cement to create a stunning, sparkly surface. The quartz crystals catch light and create a shimmering effect that's truly eye-catching.

Quartz finishes are more durable than standard plaster and offer better stain resistance. They're an excellent middle-ground option between budget plaster and premium Pebble Tec.

Benefits of Quartz

  • Beautiful sparkle: Quartz crystals create stunning light effects

Tile & Coping Repair

Complete the look with waterline tile and coping restoration

Waterline Tile Replacement

The waterline tile around your pool takes a beating from sun, chemicals, and water level changes. Cracked, faded, or missing tiles detract from your pool's appearance and can allow water to seep behind the tile.

We remove old tile, repair the underlying surface, and install new waterline tile that complements your resurfaced pool. Choose from ceramic, porcelain, glass, or stone tile options.

Coping Stone Repair

Coping stones form the edge of your pool deck. Over time, they can crack, shift, or become loose. Damaged coping is both unsightly and potentially dangerous.

We repair or replace coping stones to restore safety and beauty. Options include concrete, travertine, brick, and decorative pavers.

Why Replace Tile During Resurfacing?

  • Pool is already drained - save on labor costs
  • New tile complements your fresh surface
  • Address all issues at once
  • Complete transformation of your pool

Pool Draining & Acid Wash

Deep cleaning to restore your pool's appearance

When Acid Washing Helps

If your pool surface is stained but structurally sound, an acid wash may restore its appearance without full resurfacing. This process removes a thin layer of plaster to reveal fresh material underneath.

Acid washing is effective for:

  • Mineral stains and calcium deposits
  • Algae staining
  • General discoloration
  • Preparing surface for painting

Important Considerations

Acid washing removes a small amount of plaster each time, so it can only be done a limited number of times before resurfacing becomes necessary. We'll inspect your pool and give honest advice about whether acid washing is appropriate or if resurfacing is the better investment.

Our Process

  • Properly drain pool to avoid structural damage
  • Apply acid solution in sections
  • Neutralize and rinse thoroughly
  • Refill and balance water chemistry

Acid Wash Pricing

$400 - $800 for most residential pools

Includes draining, acid wash, and refill.

Pool Resurfacing Cost in Phoenix: 2026 Complete Price Guide

Everything you need to know about pool resurfacing costs in the Phoenix area, from budget plaster to premium Pebble Tec.

If you're a Phoenix homeowner wondering "how much does pool resurfacing cost?" you're not alone. It's one of the most common questions we receive, and the answer depends on several factors including pool size, finish type, and current condition.

In this guide, we'll break down the costs for every type of pool finish available in the Phoenix area so you can budget appropriately for your project.

Quick Cost Summary

Finish Type Cost per Sq Ft 400 sq ft Pool Lifespan
Standard Plaster $4 - $6 $1,600 - $2,400 7-12 years
Quartz Aggregate $6 - $9 $2,400 - $3,600 10-15 years
Pebble Tec $8 - $12 $3,200 - $4,800 15-20+ years

Plaster Pool Resurfacing Cost

Standard pool plaster is the most affordable option at $4-6 per square foot. For an average 400 square foot pool, expect to pay $1,600 - $2,400 for a complete plaster resurface.

Plaster provides the classic white pool look and has been the industry standard for decades. In Arizona's harsh climate, plaster typically lasts 7-12 years before needing replacement.

Plaster Pros:

  • Lowest upfront cost
  • Classic, timeless appearance
  • Smooth texture underfoot
  • Easy to patch if damaged

Plaster Cons:

  • Shortest lifespan in Arizona climate
  • More susceptible to staining
  • Requires more maintenance
  • Surface can become rough over time

Pebble Tec Pool Resurfacing Cost

Pebble Tec is the premium choice for Arizona pools at $8-12 per square foot. For a 400 square foot pool, budget $3,200 - $4,800.

While Pebble Tec costs more upfront, it typically lasts 15-20+ years in Arizona conditions - often twice as long as standard plaster. This makes it a better long-term value for many homeowners.

Pebble Tec Pros:

  • Superior durability (15-20+ years)
  • Excellent stain resistance
  • Wide range of colors available
  • Better handles Arizona's hard water
  • Lower lifetime cost

Pebble Tec Cons:

  • Higher upfront cost
  • Slightly rougher texture than plaster
  • More difficult to repair if damaged

Quartz Pool Finish Cost

Quartz finishes offer a middle ground at $6-9 per square foot. For a 400 square foot pool, expect $2,400 - $3,600.

Quartz combines crusite quartz aggregate with white cement, creating a beautiful sparkle effect while providing better durability than standard plaster.

Factors That Affect Pool Resurfacing Cost

Pebble Tec vs Plaster: Which Pool Finish is Right for You?

A comprehensive comparison of Arizona's two most popular pool finishes to help you make the right choice for your budget and lifestyle.

When it's time to resurface your Phoenix pool, the most important decision you'll make is choosing the right finish. The two most popular options in Arizona are standard plaster and Pebble Tec (pebble aggregate). Both have loyal supporters, but which one is actually right for your pool?

After resurfacing hundreds of pools in the Phoenix area, we've helped homeowners navigate this exact decision. Here's everything you need to know to make an informed choice.

Quick Comparison

Factor Plaster Pebble Tec
Cost $4-6/sq ft $8-12/sq ft
Lifespan 7-12 years 15-20+ years
Texture Smooth Textured (varies)
Colors Limited (whites, blues) 50+ options
Stain Resistance Moderate Excellent
Chemical Resistance Moderate Excellent

Understanding Plaster Pool Finishes

Pool plaster has been the standard finish for decades. It's a mixture of white cement, marble dust (or limestone), and water that creates a smooth, classic look.

Plaster Advantages

  • Lower upfront cost: At $4-6 per square foot, plaster is 40-50% cheaper than Pebble Tec initially
  • Smooth texture: Many swimmers prefer the silky feel of plaster underfoot
  • Classic appearance: The traditional white pool look that never goes out of style
  • Easy repairs: Small cracks and chips can be patched relatively easily
  • Proven track record: Decades of reliable performance when properly maintained

Plaster Disadvantages

  • Shorter lifespan: Typically needs replacement every 7-12 years in Arizona
  • Staining susceptibility: More prone to mineral staining from Arizona's hard water
  • Surface degradation: Can become rough and uncomfortable over time
  • Color limitations: Primarily available in white and light blue shades
  • More maintenance: Requires consistent chemical balance to prevent etching

Understanding Pebble Tec Finishes

Pebble Tec is a brand name that's become synonymous with pebble aggregate finishes. It combines small, smooth pebbles with cement to create a durable, textured surface with a natural appearance.

Pebble Tec Advantages

  • Superior durability: Lasts 15-20+ years, often twice as long as plaster
  • Excellent stain resistance: The aggregate surface hides and resists staining
  • Color variety: Over 50 colors and blends to match any backyard aesthetic
  • Handles hard water: Better resistance to Arizona's mineral-heavy water
  • Lower lifetime cost: Despite higher upfront cost, longer lifespan = better value
  • Unique appearance: Natural, upscale look that enhances home value

Pebble Tec Disadvantages

  • Higher initial cost: $8-12 per square foot is a significant investment
  • Texture concerns: Some find the surface too rough, especially for children
  • Complex repairs: Patches are more visible and difficult to blend
  • Break-in period: New Pebble Tec can feel rough until it wears in (2-4 weeks)

Which Finish Wins in Arizona?

Here's the honest truth: Pebble Tec performs better in Arizona's climate. Our intense sun, extreme heat, and notoriously hard water are tough on pool surfaces. Pebble Tec's durability gives it a significant advantage in these conditions.

However, that doesn't mean plaster is a bad choice. Here's when each finish makes sense:

Choose Plaster If:

  • You're on a tight budget and need the lowest upfront cost
  • You prefer a completely smooth surface for swimming
  • You're selling the home within 5-7 years
  • You have young children who play on pool steps frequently
  • You want the classic white pool aesthetic

Choose Pebble Tec If:

  • You plan to stay in your home long-term (10+ years)
  • You want minimal maintenance and maximum durability
  • You prefer natural, earth-tone aesthetics
  • Your pool has persistent staining issues
  • You want the best long-term value

The Cost Reality Check

Let's do the math for a typical 400 sq ft pool:

Plaster: $2,000 initial × 2 applications over 20 years = $4,000 total

Pebble Tec: $4,000 initial × 1 application over 20 years = $4,000 total

Over a 20-year period, the costs are nearly identical. But with Pebble Tec, you avoid the hassle, downtime, and potential price increases of a second resurfacing project.

What About Quartz Finishes?

If you're torn between plaster and Pebble Tec, quartz finishes offer a middle ground. At $6-9 per square foot, quartz provides better durability than plaster with a smoother texture than Pebble Tec.

5 Signs Your Pool Needs Resurfacing (Don't Ignore #3)

Learn the warning signs that indicate it's time to resurface your Phoenix pool before minor issues become major expenses.

Your pool's surface doesn't fail overnight. It deteriorates gradually, and many homeowners don't realize there's a problem until they're dealing with leaks, injuries, or equipment damage. Knowing the warning signs can save you thousands of dollars and keep your family safe.

Here are the five most common signs that your Phoenix pool needs resurfacing—and why ignoring them can be costly.

1. Rough, Sandpaper-Like Texture

What it looks like: When you run your hand along the pool surface, it feels like sandpaper instead of smooth plaster.

Why it happens: Arizona's hard water and intense UV exposure cause the cement in your pool finish to erode, exposing the rough aggregate underneath.

Why you shouldn't ignore it:

  • Swimmers can scrape their feet, knees, and hands
  • Bathing suits snag and wear out faster
  • Algae finds more places to hide, making cleaning harder
  • The rough texture indicates ongoing deterioration

Urgency level: Medium. You have time to plan, but don't wait more than 1-2 seasons.

2. Persistent Staining That Won't Clean

What it looks like: Brown, blue, green, or rust-colored stains that don't respond to normal cleaning, acid washing, or stain treatments.

Why it happens: As pool plaster ages, it becomes more porous. Minerals from Phoenix's hard water (copper, iron, manganese) penetrate deep into the surface where they can't be removed.

Why you shouldn't ignore it:

  • Staining will only get worse over time
  • Repeated acid washing thins the surface faster
  • Deep stains indicate a compromised surface
  • Your pool looks dirty even when it's clean

Urgency level: Low to medium. Primarily cosmetic, but indicates surface degradation.

3. Plaster Flaking, Peeling, or Delaminating

What it looks like: Chunks of plaster coming off in your hand, white flakes floating in the water, or sections where the surface is visibly separating from the shell.

Why it happens: This is called delamination, and it occurs when the bond between the plaster and the concrete shell fails. In Arizona, this is often caused by improper initial application, chemical imbalances, or simply age.

Why you shouldn't ignore it:

  • This is the most serious sign—delamination accelerates rapidly
  • Loose plaster can clog your filter and damage pumps
  • Exposed concrete is vulnerable to water damage
  • Structural damage becomes possible if ignored
  • Repair costs increase significantly the longer you wait

Urgency level: HIGH. Address this within weeks, not months. Get a professional assessment immediately.

4. Visible Cracks (Beyond Hairline)

What it looks like: Cracks wider than a hairline, cracks that have expanded over time, or cracks you can feel with your fingernail.

Why it happens: All pools develop some hairline cracks due to ground movement and temperature changes. However, larger cracks often indicate structural movement or a failing surface.

Why you shouldn't ignore it:

  • Cracks can allow water to reach the concrete shell
  • Freeze-thaw cycles (yes, even in Phoenix) can expand cracks
  • Algae and bacteria thrive in cracks
  • Cracks wider than 1/8" may indicate structural issues

Urgency level: Medium to high, depending on crack size and location. Cracks near fittings or on the floor are more concerning.

5. Consistent Water Chemistry Problems

What it looks like: You're constantly battling pH levels, adding excessive chemicals, or your water just never seems balanced despite your best efforts.

Why it happens: As pool plaster deteriorates, it releases calcium and other minerals into the water, throwing off your chemistry. The porous surface also absorbs chemicals unevenly.

Why you shouldn't ignore it:

  • You're wasting money on excess chemicals
  • Unbalanced water accelerates surface deterioration
  • Equipment (heaters, pumps, salt cells) gets damaged
  • Swimmer comfort and safety are affected

Urgency level: Medium. Often overlooked, but a clear sign your surface is compromised.

What Happens If You Wait Too Long?

Delaying necessary resurfacing doesn't just affect aesthetics. Here's what's at stake:

  • Higher costs: A surface that's completely failed may require bond coat repairs, adding $500-1,500 to your project
  • Equipment damage: Loose plaster and unbalanced water destroy pumps, heaters, and filters
  • Structural damage: Water penetrating the concrete shell can cause serious problems
  • Safety hazards: Rough surfaces cause injuries; loose plaster is a slip hazard
  • Pool closure: Severe deterioration may require draining—risky in Arizona's expansive soils
