If you're a Phoenix homeowner wondering "how much does pool resurfacing cost?" you're not alone. It's one of the most common questions we receive, and the answer depends on several factors including pool size, finish type, and current condition.

In this guide, we'll break down the costs for every type of pool finish available in the Phoenix area so you can budget appropriately for your project.

Quick Cost Summary

Finish Type Cost per Sq Ft 400 sq ft Pool Lifespan

Plaster Pool Resurfacing Cost

Standard pool plaster is the most affordable option at $4-6 per square foot. For an average 400 square foot pool, expect to pay $1,600 - $2,400 for a complete plaster resurface.

Plaster provides the classic white pool look and has been the industry standard for decades. In Arizona's harsh climate, plaster typically lasts 7-12 years before needing replacement.

Plaster Pros:

  • Lowest upfront cost
  • Classic, timeless appearance
  • Smooth texture underfoot
  • Easy to patch if damaged

Plaster Cons:

  • Shortest lifespan in Arizona climate
  • More susceptible to staining
  • Requires more maintenance
  • Surface can become rough over time

Pebble Tec Pool Resurfacing Cost

Pebble Tec is the premium choice for Arizona pools at $8-12 per square foot. For a 400 square foot pool, budget $3,200 - $4,800.

While Pebble Tec costs more upfront, it typically lasts 15-20+ years in Arizona conditions - often twice as long as standard plaster. This makes it a better long-term value for many homeowners.

Pebble Tec Pros:

  • Superior durability (15-20+ years)
  • Excellent stain resistance
  • Wide range of colors available
  • Better handles Arizona's hard water
  • Lower lifetime cost

Pebble Tec Cons:

  • Higher upfront cost
  • Slightly rougher texture than plaster
  • More difficult to repair if damaged

Quartz Pool Finish Cost

Quartz finishes offer a middle ground at $6-9 per square foot. For a 400 square foot pool, expect $2,400 - $3,600.

Quartz combines crusite quartz aggregate with white cement, creating a beautiful sparkle effect while providing better durability than standard plaster.

Factors That Affect Pool Resurfacing Cost

  • Pool size: Larger pools cost more but have a lower per-square-foot cost
  • Current condition: Extensive repairs add to the base cost
  • Tile and coping: Replacing waterline tile adds $15-35 per linear foot
  • Deck condition: Deck resurfacing is often done simultaneously
  • Access: Difficult access can increase labor costs

When to Resurface Your Pool

Signs your pool needs resurfacing include:

  • Rough, sandpaper-like texture
  • Visible staining that won't clean
  • Plaster flaking or peeling
  • Consistent water chemistry problems
  • Visible cracks or hollow spots
