How We Calculate Your Pool Resurfacing Cost

Our calculator uses 2026 pricing data from the Phoenix metro area to give you an accurate estimate. We factor in your pool's interior surface area (not just length x width - pools have walls too), your chosen finish material, and any additional services.

The surface area multiplier accounts for average pool depth, wall coverage, and benches or steps. A typical 30x15 pool has approximately 900 square feet of interior surface to coat.

Factors That Affect Your Final Price

Pool condition: Pools with significant structural damage, delamination, or rebar exposure require more prep work, which adds $500-$2,000 to the project.

Pool size and shape: Freeform pools with curves, benches, or spa attachments have more surface area than rectangular pools of the same footprint.

Access and location: Difficult backyard access requiring hand-carrying materials can add $300-$800. Hillside properties may have additional costs.

Arizona hard water: Phoenix's mineral-heavy water is tough on pool surfaces. This is why many homeowners upgrade from plaster to more durable Pebble Tec or quartz finishes during resurfacing.

Is It Worth Upgrading to Pebble Tec?

While Pebble Tec costs roughly twice as much as plaster upfront, it lasts nearly twice as long. When you calculate cost per year, the difference shrinks significantly. Use the calculator above to compare - the "cost per year" figure tells the real story. Read our full Pebble Tec vs Plaster comparison for more details.
