What is Pool Plaster?

Pool plaster is a cement-based finish that has been the industry standard for over 50 years. It's made from white Portland cement mixed with marble dust or limestone, creating a smooth, waterproof surface.

The classic white color reflects sunlight beautifully, making your pool water appear bright and inviting. In Arizona's climate, properly maintained plaster typically lasts 7-12 years before needing replacement.

Plaster FAQs

How long does plaster last in Arizona?
With proper chemical maintenance, pool plaster typically lasts 7-12 years in Arizona. Our harsh sun and hard water can be tough on surfaces, so consistent care is important. We provide aftercare instructions to help maximize your plaster's lifespan.
Is plaster or Pebble Tec better?
It depends on your priorities. Plaster is more affordable upfront and has a smoother texture. Pebble Tec costs more but lasts nearly twice as long. For budget-focused projects or if you're selling soon, plaster is often the smart choice. Read our full comparison guide.
How long until I can swim after resurfacing?
You'll typically be swimming within 7-10 days. After application, we fill the pool and begin the chemistry balancing process. The plaster needs about a week to cure properly before swimming is safe.
Can you change the color of my plaster?
Yes! While white is classic, we also offer colored plaster options including light blue, gray, and other shades. Colored plaster can create beautiful water effects and match your backyard aesthetic.
