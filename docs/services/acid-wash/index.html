When Acid Washing Helps

If your pool surface is stained but structurally sound, an acid wash can restore its appearance without full resurfacing. The process removes a thin layer to reveal fresh material underneath.

Good for: Mineral stains, calcium deposits, algae staining, general discoloration.

Acid Wash FAQs

How often should you acid wash a pool?
Most pools benefit from an acid wash every 3-5 years, or whenever staining becomes noticeable. In Arizona, calcium buildup from hard water is the most common reason for acid washing.
Is acid washing safe for my pool?
Yes, when done by professionals. We use carefully diluted muriatic acid and proven techniques to remove stains without damaging the underlying surface. We also neutralize and dispose of waste water properly.
What's the difference between acid wash and resurfacing?
An acid wash removes surface stains and deposits from your existing finish — it's a deep clean. Resurfacing replaces the entire pool surface with new material. Acid washing is less expensive but doesn't fix structural issues like cracks or rough texture.
How long does an acid wash take?
A standard acid wash takes 1-2 days including draining, washing, and refilling. It's the quickest way to dramatically improve your pool's appearance.
